Ohm's Law & Power

V = I × R | I = V ÷ R | R = V ÷ I P = V × I | P = I² × R | P = V² ÷ R

Circuits

Series: Current same throughout. Voltage splits. R = R1 + R2 + R3
Parallel: Voltage same across all branches. Current splits. 1/R = 1/R1 + 1/R2 + 1/R3. Total R always less than smallest branch.

Shock Protection

Class I: Earthed metalwork — relies on earth conductor
Class II: Double insulated — no earth required. Symbol: square within a square
Class III: Operates at SELV — max 50V AC / 120V DC ripple-free
SELV: Separated Extra Low Voltage — isolated from earth and other circuits
PELV: Protected Extra Low Voltage — connected to earth
RCD: 30mA personal protection | 100mA or 300mA fire and equipment

Earthing Arrangements

TN-S: Separate neutral and earth throughout
TN-C-S (PME): Combined PEN splits at cut-out — most common UK domestic
TT: Earth electrode at premises — no metallic return path

Protective Devices

Fuse: Single use — melts to break circuit
MCB: Resettable — trips on overload or short circuit
RCBO: Combined RCD + MCB
Voltage drop: 3% lighting / 5% power

Safe Isolation (8 steps)

1. Identify circuit | 2. Confirm convenient to isolate | 3. Select approved VI; test on proving unit | 4. Verify circuit LIVE | 5. Isolate at CU | 6. Verify circuit DEAD; recheck on live supply | 7. Lock off; keep fuse in pocket | 8. Post warning notice
GS38: Approved test leads required throughout

Cable Colours

Post-2004: Brown = Line | Blue = Neutral | Green/Yellow = Earth
Old: Red = Live | Black = Neutral | Green = Earth

Site Voltages

25V Violet — confined lighting | 110V Yellow — tools | 230V Blue — offices via RCD | 400V Red — fixed 3-phase

Heat Energy

Q = m × c × ΔT c (water) = 4.187 kJ/kg·K
Sensible heat: Causes temperature change — no change of state
Latent heat: Causes change of state — no temperature change during transition

Pressure & Head

Pressure = Force ÷ Area (Pa = N/m²) 1m head = 9.81 kPa (~10 kPa practical) Absolute = gauge + 101.325 kPa
Static: Water not moving | Dynamic: Flowing — always lower than static
Gauge pressure: Measured above atmospheric
Pascal's Law: Pressure acts equally in all directions in a liquid

Gas Laws

Boyle's: P1V1 = P2V2 (constant temp) Charles's: V1/T1 = V2/T2 (constant pressure)
Gases: compressible, expand to fill container | Liquids: incompressible

Thermal Expansion

Expansion = length × coefficient × temp rise Copper: 0.000017/°C

Material Properties

Ferrous: Contains iron — rusts | Non-ferrous: No iron — corrosion resistant
Ductile: Drawn into wire/bent without breaking (copper)
Malleable: Shaped by hammering (lead)
Tensile strength: Resistance to being pulled apart
Compressive: Resistance to being crushed
Electrolytic corrosion: Two dissimilar metals + water
Dezincification: Zinc leaches from brass in hard water — use DZR fittings

LPHW & Density

LPHW differential: 20°C flow/return typical
Water: 1000 kg/m³ | RD 1.0 | Volume = mass ÷ density
RD > 1 sinks | RD < 1 floats

Copper Pipe Grades (BS EN 1057)

R220: Soft/annealed, thick wall — coils, microbore, underground
R250: Half-hard — most common above-ground plumbing and heating
R290: Hard temper, thin wall — rarely used in UK

Pipe Bending

Pipe gain: Subtract from measured length to get correct cut length
15mm 90° ≈ 4mm gain | 22mm 90° ≈ 6mm gain

Jointing

Type A compression: Olive seals — no PTFE, no pipe mod
Type B compression: Pipe end flared — underground use
End feed: Solder applied separately | Solder ring: Pre-loaded solder
Flux: Cleans + prevents oxidation. Clean excess off after — causes corrosion.
Lead-free solder mandatory for domestic water
Push-fit: Pipe inserts required in plastic pipe
BSP thread: PTFE on male thread only, wound in direction of thread

Joist Notching & Drilling

Notches: Top, 0.1–0.2 of span from support. Max depth = 1/8 joist depth
Holes: Middle third, 0.25–0.4 of span. Max dia = 1/4 depth. Min spacing = 3 × dia

Plastic Pipes

uPVC: Cold water/waste/soil — max ~60°C, NOT hot water
PEX: Flexible, shape memory, underfloor heating, hot & cold
Polybutylene (PB): Flexible, hot & cold, heating
MDPE Blue: Cold water mains underground
MDPE Yellow: Gas underground — butt fusion, electrofusion or compression

Key Legislation

HASAWA 1974: S.2 safe systems. S.3 protect non-employees. S.7 employees take reasonable care. S.8 no horseplay.
RIDDOR 2013: 3-day injuries within 15 days. Deaths immediately.
COSHH 2002: 8 steps. Check data sheet before use.
Work at Height 2005: Avoid → Prevent → Minimise
CDM 2015: H&S file stays with building until demolition.
Acts = Mandatory | ACOPs = evidence of guilt if prosecuted | Guidance = advisory

Risk Assessment

1. Identify hazards | 2. Who harmed & how | 3. Evaluate & decide precautions | 4. Record & implement | 5. Review & update
Risk = Likelihood × Consequence (1–5 each, max 25) 1–5 Low | 6–12 Medium | 13–25 High — stop work

Fire Extinguishers

Water: A | Foam: A+B | Dry Powder: A B C E | CO₂: B C E | Wet Chemical: F only
PASS: Pull → Aim base → Squeeze → Sweep. 3m back.

PPE Standards

Hard hat BS EN 397 | Footwear EN 20345 | Eye EN 166/172 | Mechanical gloves EN 388 | Thermal EN 407 | Chemical EN 374 | Hearing BS EN 352 (>80 dB(A))

Excavations

Max unsupported: 1.2m. Spoil min 1m from edge (fixed). NO propane in trenches.

Working at Height

Ladders: 75° / 4:1. Extend 1m above. Tie if >3m.
Tower: Indoor 3.5× base. Outdoor 3×. Tie >6.4m. Never move with people on.

Hazardous Materials

Asbestos: STOP → WITHDRAW → REPORT → Licensed contractor. Pre-2000 buildings.
Diseases: Asbestosis | Pleural Disease | Mesothelioma (30–40yr) | Lung Cancer
LPG: Upright, ventilated, outdoors/cage. NEVER below ground or boiler rooms. 3m from O₂.
Acetylene: Max 15 PSI. Work 5–8 PSI. Flashback arresters on BOTH lines.
Oxygen: Black cylinder, right-hand thread. No oil/grease. 3m from LPG.

First Aid

CPR: 30:2 at 100–120/min. 4–5cm depth.
Burns: 10 min cold water. No cream. Don't burst blisters.
Electric shock: Never touch if in contact. Isolate first.

Key Roles

Client: Commissions work, signs off VOs
Architect: Designs, produces drawings
QS: Controls costs, bills of quantities, values variations
Clerk of Works: Client's on-site rep — work to agreed plans
BCO: Local authority — building regs compliance at key stages

CDM 2015

Notifiable: 30+ days AND 20+ simultaneous workers, OR 500+ person-days
H&S file stays with building until demolition

Key Documents

RAMS: Operatives must sign before work begins
Variation Order: Formal written, client signs BEFORE work proceeds
Delivery Note: No pricing — supplier, date, qty, description, order ref only
Gantt chart: Work programme — sequence, timing, duration
Snagging list: Defects at completion, rectified before handover
As-built drawings: Final record of what was actually installed

Legislation Status

BS standards: Advisory — enforceability comes from legislation referencing them
Equality Act 2010: 9 protected characteristics
FOIA 2000: Info from public bodies | DPA 2018: Personal data

Communication & Authority

Communication has only worked when it has been understood
Formal quotations MUST be in writing
ACAS: Free impartial employment mediation
Level 2: Carry out work as instructed. Cannot authorise variations.
